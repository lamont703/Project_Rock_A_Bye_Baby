The Timeline

17 Years of Legal Battles: A Complete Chronology of Constitutional Violations and Systemic Injustice

2008-2010 2011-2013 2013-2015 2019 2025

Initial Custody Dispute & Child Support Enforcement

2008 - 2010

The Beginning

December 2008

Argument between Lamont Evans and Talicia Townsend leads to her moving in with her parents, taking the children. Both parties remained in contact discussing potential reconciliation, believing co-parenting could work.

Separation Children Relocated

Relationship Ends

March 2009

Talicia Townsend decided to be with another man, which marked the definitive end of their romantic relationship.

Relationship End Infidelity

Mediation Agreement

May 2009

Lamont Evans requested mediation to establish a fixed parenting schedule. Both parties agreed that the children would reside with Lamont every other Sunday through Thursday.

Mediation Parenting Schedule

Child Support Filed

October 2009

Talicia Townsend filed for child support from Lamont Evans. Lamont suggested modifying custody to 50/50 to avoid using the child support enforcement agency, but Talicia refused and continued pursuing financial support despite shared parenting time.

Child Support Filing 50/50 Custody Rejected

Child Support Calculation

Late 2009

Child support initially calculated at $850/month. After Lamont's request for adjustment citing shared parenting time, Talicia agreed to lower it to $407/month plus 2% processing fee. Lamont again offered equal custody to eliminate child support, but was refused.

Support Reduction Equal Custody Refused

Paternity Established

February 2010

The paternity of Lamont Evans' children was legally established during the child support process, formalizing his legal status as their father.

Paternity Legal Process

First Legal Action

May 2010

Child support order of $407/month plus 2% processing fee was finalized. Lamont Evans filed his first lawsuit against Talicia Townsend to seek shared parenting, beginning what would become a 17-year legal battle.

Child Support Order First Lawsuit Filed

The Legal Battles Begin

2011 - 2013

Civil Protection Order Filed

January 2011

Lamont Evans filed a civil protection order (CPO) against Talicia Townsend, citing harassment, threats, and behavior that disrupted stability for the children, including coming to Lamont's home and causing police involvement.

CPO Filed Harassment

CPO Case Dismissed

January 2011

Talicia Townsend failed to appear at the CPO hearing. Despite having grounds to proceed, Lamont chose to drop the case to avoid jeopardizing her nursing career and licensing, hoping this would encourage better behavior.

Case Dismissed Career Consideration

Attorney Retained

June 2012

Lamont Evans retained an attorney to assist with pursuing shared custody of his children. This marked the beginning of formal legal representation in his fight for equal parenting rights.

Legal Representation Shared Custody

Shared Custody Lawsuit Filed

July 2012

Lamont Evans filed a lawsuit against Talicia Townsend for shared parenting custody, requesting equal parenting time with the children. This formal legal action sought to establish 50/50 custody.

Lawsuit Filed Equal Parenting

Court Investigation

October 2012

A court investigator was assigned to evaluate the children's safety and well-being in both households. The investigator found that the children were safe, sound, and in good condition with both sides of the family.

Court Investigation Children Safe

Shared Custody Denied

December 7, 2012

Magistrate Darrolyn Krippel ruled to deny Lamont Evans shared parenting custody. During preliminary hearing, Talicia initially agreed to equal parenting time until learning it would eliminate child support, then objected. Denial was based on Talicia's objection and attorney's failure to file formal shared parenting plan.

Custody Denied Financial Motivation

Child Support Enforcement Intensifies

2013

As Lamont continued paying attorney fees, CSEA intensified enforcement actions, threatening suspension of driver's license, revocation of barber's license, loss of passport, fines, penalties, and possible jail time—all without due process or finding of unfitness.

Enforcement Escalation Due Process Violations

Letter to Talicia

January 27, 2013

Lamont wrote a formal letter to Talicia explaining his position and requesting equal parenting. He outlined financial hardship from $407/month child support, offered 50/50 custody split with no support payments, and set February 25, 2013 deadline for response before proceeding with litigation.

Formal Letter Settlement Attempt

Mediation Breakdown

July 2013

Court-ordered mediation attempted to reach custody agreement. During mediation, Talicia openly admitted she was refusing equal custody because she wanted to continue collecting child support payments—acknowledging that preventing equal parenting time was her only way to receive financial support.

Mediation Failure Financial Motivation Admitted

Constitutional Violations in Court

June 2013

Trial transcripts show Talicia had no valid reason to withhold shared parenting. Magistrate Darrolyn Krippel denied request solely on technicality—no formal shared parenting plan filed by attorney—despite no legal reason presented. Attorney's mistake cost $600 more with no guarantee, violating Lamont's and children's constitutional rights.

Constitutional Violations Attorney Malpractice

Escalation, Alienation & Federal Violations

2013 - 2015

Final Custody Ruling - Rights Violated

June 2013

Magistrate Darrolyn Krippel ruled that Talicia would be sole residential parent and legal custodian, granting Lamont only 5 days of parenting time out of 14. This clearly violated Lamont's and his children's constitutional rights to equal parenting relationship despite no legal justification for denial.

Limited Custody Constitutional Violation

Continued Financial Burden

2013-2015

Despite having court-ordered visitation 5 out of 14 days, Lamont continued paying over $400/month in child support plus accumulated arrears from legal expenses. CSEA continued threatening driver's license suspension, barber's license revocation, wage garnishment, and jail time.

Financial Strain Enforcement Threats

CSEA Employee's Compassion

2015

A woman from CSEA called to understand why Lamont had fallen behind on payments. After hearing his story, she began to cry and admitted the system had treated him unfairly. She pleaded with him to take the matter back to court and represent himself (pro se), providing guidance on legal research and filing procedures.

System Insider Support Legal Guidance

Pro Se Motion Filed

July 2015

Lamont filed a motion with the Clerk of Courts for case reconsideration. Strategically requesting mediation first, he sought to increase parenting time from 5 to 6 days out of 14. Talicia agreed to one extra day only if child support continued at current rate.

Pro Se Filing Strategic Mediation

Equal Custody Finally Achieved

Late 2015

New magistrate (Magistrate Reedus) rejected Talicia's objection to equal parenting time, ruling that both parents would be legal custodians with 7 days on/7 days off schedule. Child support reduced to $113.75 medical support. However, magistrate denied request to remove arrears, leaving Lamont responsible for past accumulation despite achieving equal custody.

Equal Custody Granted Arrears Remain

Breaking Point & Relocation

2019

Secret Court Action

Early 2019

While operating under shared parenting plan, Talicia secretly went to court requesting child support modification—without notifying Lamont. He never received legal notice that a modification case had been filed, violating his due process rights.

Secret Filing Due Process Violation

Child Support Drastically Increased

August 2019

Lamont received unexpected call from CSEA informing him his child support obligation increased to $900/month despite existing shared parenting agreement. CSEA confirmed Talicia had obtained the modification and told him the only way to change it was to take her back to court himself.

$900/Month Shared Custody Ignored

Daughter's Mental Health Crisis

July 2019

Lamont's 16-year-old daughter confided that she couldn't wait to become an adult so she could leave both parents. She was experiencing severe mental anxiety and depression due to ongoing parental conflict. This revelation made Lamont realize another court battle would severely harm his children's mental and emotional health.

Mental Health Crisis Children's Suffering

Heartbreaking Decision

August 2019

When Lamont told Talicia he wouldn't take her back to court because of their children's well-being, she coldly responded it was not her problem and he should find a way to deal with it. Forced to choose between legal fight and children's mental stability, Lamont chose to protect them.

Children First Painful Sacrifice

Final Goodbye

Late August 2019

Lamont sat down with his children and explained why he couldn't continue their equal parenting schedule—their mother had changed things in court. He told them he couldn't keep fighting their mother because it wasn't in their best interest. They all cried together for a long time before he took them back to their mother's house.

Final Explanation Family Tears

Move to Georgia

September 2019

A couple weeks later, Lamont moved to Georgia for a new job opportunity that had been offered just two weeks prior. This move marked the beginning of deeper separation from his children—not by choice, but by necessity to protect their mental and emotional well-being from further legal battles.

Relocation New Chapter

Legal Strategy & Public Awareness

2025

Children Now Adults - Ongoing Suffering

2025

It has been incredibly difficult explaining past events to the children, who continue suffering from anxiety, depression, and resentment toward their father because they don't fully understand what happened. The youngest son is now 17 and about to graduate high school, marking a new chapter as children reach adulthood.

Children's Mental Health Adult Transition

Preparing Legal Action - Multiple Defendants

2025

With children now reaching adulthood, Lamont prepares comprehensive legal case against: Talicia Townsend for alienating children and using them for financial gain, her husband for culpability in reinforcing alienation, and the State of Ohio for enabling systematic violation of parental rights and denying children's right to equal relationship with their father.

Multi-Party Lawsuit Parental Alienation

Evidence of Husband's Role in Alienation

2025

Notable incident highlighting husband's involvement: When Lamont was picking up children to take them to school bus stop, Talicia came outside and said her husband didn't want Lamont stopping in front of their house and wanted him to park down the street instead. This clearly demonstrated effort to alienate Lamont from his own children, making him feel like an outsider despite legal parenting rights.

Alienation Evidence Husband's Culpability

Project Rock A Bye Baby Launches

2025

This platform launches to share the complete story, raise public awareness, and provide resources for other parents facing similar injustices. The platform serves as both digital testimony and catalyst for systemic reform, ensuring the truth is revealed and no other family endures similar injustice.

Public Platform Truth and Accountability Justice for Children
