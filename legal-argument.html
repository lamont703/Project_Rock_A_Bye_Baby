I. Introduction

This case is brought forth to rectify years of constitutional violations, parental alienation, and systemic abuse by Talicia Townsend, her husband, and the State of Ohio. Through a series of deliberate and coordinated actions, these parties have denied me (Lamont Evans) my fundamental parental rights, alienated my children from me for financial gain, and subjected me to unconstitutional state enforcement actions that have caused irreparable harm to me and my children.

This legal argument will establish:

  1. Constitutional Violations committed by the State of Ohio.
  2. Culpability of Talicia Townsend in engaging in parental alienation and financial exploitation.
  3. Culpability of Talicia's Husband in reinforcing alienation and restricting my parental rights.

II. Theory of the Case: Legal and Factual Justifications

The theory of this case is built upon the fundamental constitutional rights of a parent to maintain a meaningful relationship with their children without unjust interference from the other parent or the state.

This case seeks to establish that:

  1. The State of Ohio (via the Family Court and Child Support Enforcement Agency) violated constitutional rights through unfair legal procedures and financial penalties, effectively punishing a fit and willing father.
  2. Talicia Townsend engaged in a pattern of parental alienation and financial exploitation, using child support as leverage to deny equal parenting.
  3. Talicia's husband played an active role in reinforcing and enabling the alienation, creating further obstacles to a healthy father-child relationship.

By applying the relevant legal standards to the factual record, this case will prove that these parties acted unlawfully and unconstitutionally, causing significant harm to both the plaintiff (Lamont Evans) and his children.

The Legal Framework Supporting Our Claims

The case rests on three key legal principles:

1. The Fundamental Right to Parent (Fourteenth Amendment)

Legal Standard: The U.S. Supreme Court has long recognized parental rights as a fundamental liberty interest under the Fourteenth Amendment's Due Process Clause (Troxel v. Granville, 530 U.S. 57, 2000).

Application to This Case:

  • The State of Ohio interfered with my parental rights without proper due process (e.g., child support increases without notice, unjust custody decisions).
  • The court denied my request for shared parenting in 2013 based on a procedural issue (failure of my attorney to file paperwork) rather than an actual evaluation of my fitness as a parent.
  • Talicia actively interfered with my parental rights for financial gain, which the state enabled by enforcing unfair child support rulings.

2. Due Process Violations (Fourteenth Amendment)

Legal Standard: Due process requires that before the government takes away property or liberty, an individual must have notice and an opportunity to be heard (Mathews v. Eldridge, 424 U.S. 319, 1976).

Application to This Case:

  • In 2019, the State of Ohio increased my child support without notifying me or giving me a chance to contest it.
  • The child support system imposed penalties (license suspensions, wage garnishments) without allowing me to present a defense or argue my financial situation.
  • I was denied a fair hearing before being subjected to financial punishment.

3. Equal Protection Violations (Fourteenth Amendment)

Legal Standard: The Equal Protection Clause requires that similarly situated individuals be treated equally under the law (Stanley v. Illinois, 405 U.S. 645, 1972).

Application to This Case:

  • I was subjected to discriminatory treatment based on my status as a father, as opposed to how a mother in my position would have been treated.
  • After being granted shared custody in 2015, I was still treated as a "non-custodial" parent for financial enforcement purposes, whereas a mother with equal custody would not have been required to pay child support in the same manner.

Factual Narrative Supporting These Legal Theories

1. The State of Ohio Violated Due Process & Equal Protection

2013 Custody Case:
  • Despite my active involvement in my children's lives, the court denied me shared parenting because my attorney failed to file a technical document.
  • This was a procedural failure—not an issue of my fitness as a parent—yet it resulted in the loss of my equal rights.
2019 Child Support Modification Without Notice:
  • Talicia went to court in secret to request an increase in child support without notifying me.
  • The court approved her request without giving me a chance to present my financial circumstances or contest the decision.
  • This violated my due process rights, as I was deprived of my property (financial resources) without a fair hearing.
Child Support Enforcement Without Consideration of Parenting Time:
  • Even after achieving equal parenting rights in 2015, the State of Ohio continued treating me as a non-custodial parent for child support purposes.
  • Mothers in shared parenting agreements do not face the same financial burdens, showing clear gender bias.

2. Talicia Townsend's Parental Alienation & Financial Exploitation

  • Talicia used child support as leverage to deny me equal parenting time.
    • In 2013 mediation, she admitted she didn't object to equal parenting time—until she realized she would lose child support payments.
    • She weaponized financial incentives to manipulate the legal system, prioritizing money over the children's well-being.
  • She engaged in deceptive legal actions.
    • In 2019, she went to court for a child support modification without informing me, violating my right to contest the change.
    • This was done in bad faith, knowing that I would have objected and presented evidence against the increase.
  • She knowingly caused emotional harm to the children.
    • Her refusal to co-parent effectively led to severe mental health struggles for our children, including our daughter expressing a desire to leave both parents.
    • By prioritizing financial gain over stability, she created an environment of conflict and emotional distress for our children.

3. Culpability of Talicia's Husband in Alienation

  • He actively restricted my access to my children.
    • He forbade me from stopping in front of their home to pick up my children, instead insisting I park down the street.
    • This reinforced the idea that I was not an equal parent, alienating my children from me.
  • He contributed to an environment that made co-parenting more difficult.
    • By supporting and participating in unnecessary restrictions on my parenting rights, he played a role in driving a wedge between me and my children.

Legal Theory Conclusion

This case is about more than just financial compensation—it is about justice for my children and holding accountable those who abused the system for personal gain.

  • The State of Ohio violated constitutional rights by enforcing unfair child support penalties and failing to provide due process.
  • Talicia Townsend engaged in parental alienation and financial exploitation, using child support as a weapon against me.
  • Her husband contributed to the alienation, further damaging my relationship with my children.

Now that my children are older, it is time to set the record straight and demand justice.

This legal theory connects the facts to the law in a way that establishes clear violations and justifies the relief sought.

III. Constitutional Violations by the State of Ohio

The State of Ohio, through its Child Support Enforcement Agency (CSEA), family court system, and judicial officers, has engaged in multiple violations of my constitutional rights under the Fourteenth Amendment of the U.S. Constitution, which guarantees due process and equal protection under the law.

A. Violation of the Fourteenth Amendment – Due Process Clause

The Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment prohibits the government from depriving individuals of life, liberty, or property without due process of law. My rights as a father were repeatedly violated through the following:

1. Denial of Equal Custody Without Just Cause

  • The court denied me shared parenting in 2013, not based on any evidence of unfitness, but solely because my attorney failed to file a shared parenting plan.
  • The Magistrate made a ruling based on a procedural technicality rather than the substantive rights of a fit parent.
  • This action deprived me of my fundamental liberty interest in raising my children.

2. Child Support Enforcement Actions Without Due Process

  • The State of Ohio garnished my wages, threatened my driver's license, barber's license, passport, and freedom without a fair process to consider my ability to pay.
  • These penalties were imposed without giving proper weight to the fact that I was an active and fit parent.
  • At no point was I provided a meaningful opportunity to contest the fairness of these penalties.

3. Failure to Notify Me of the 2019 Child Support Modification Hearing

  • Talicia Townsend returned to court in 2019 to request a modification of child support without notifying me, violating my right to be heard before a decision was made against me.
  • The court increased my child support to $900 per month, despite our legally binding shared parenting plan.
  • I was denied my right to due process because I was not given the opportunity to present my case before the modification was approved.

B. Violation of the Fourteenth Amendment – Equal Protection Clause

The Equal Protection Clause guarantees that similarly situated individuals must be treated equally under the law. The State of Ohio discriminated against me as a father by:

1. Favoring Talicia Townsend Despite Shared Custody

  • The court and CSEA continued enforcing full child support payments even after I was granted equal parenting time in 2015.
  • The State treated me differently than a custodial mother in the same situation, despite our shared parenting agreement.

2. Refusing to Remove Child Support Arrears Created By an Unfair System

  • The arrears accumulated because I was forced to choose between hiring an attorney for shared parenting or paying child support.
  • No custodial mother in the same position would have been placed in such a no-win financial situation.

IV. Culpability of Talicia Townsend – Parental Alienation and Financial Exploitation

Talicia Townsend knowingly and willfully engaged in behavior designed to alienate my children from me and extract financial gain, violating my parental rights and harming the emotional well-being of our children.

A. Parental Alienation

1. Withholding Shared Custody for Financial Gain

  • In 2013, Talicia openly admitted during mediation that she did not want me to have equal parenting time because she wanted to continue collecting child support.
  • She changed her testimony in court after learning that shared custody would eliminate her ability to collect payments.

2. Interfering with My Ability to Parent

  • Talicia used financial control as a weapon, refusing to negotiate any custody changes unless child support remained in place.
  • She actively made co-parenting difficult, which negatively affected our children's mental health.

B. Fraudulent Child Support Modification in 2019

  1. She returned to court in 2019 without notifying me, violating my right to contest the modification.
  2. She secured an unfair child support increase despite shared custody, forcing me into an impossible financial situation once again.
  3. She refused to reverse the modification despite my plea that it was harming our children's mental health.

V. Culpability of Talicia's Husband – Reinforcing Parental Alienation

Talicia's husband played an active role in restricting my parenting rights and reinforcing alienation through direct actions and coercive control.

A. Restricting My Access to My Children

  1. Instructing me not to stop in front of their home when picking up my children, forcing me to park down the street.
  2. Participating in and reinforcing an environment where I was treated as an outsider in my own children's lives.

B. Contributing to Emotional Harm to My Children

  1. By supporting Talicia's efforts to limit my parental involvement, he indirectly contributed to the mental and emotional distress my children endured.
  2. His actions made it more difficult for me to have a normal and healthy co-parenting relationship.

VI. Legal Relief Sought

Given the numerous constitutional violations, acts of parental alienation, and financial exploitation committed by Talicia Townsend, her husband, and the State of Ohio, I am seeking the following relief:

  1. Formal recognition of my constitutional rights being violated and acknowledgment of the harm done to me and my children.
  2. Financial compensation for the undue financial hardship and emotional distress caused by unconstitutional state enforcement actions.
  3. Restitution of unfairly accumulated child support arrears that were imposed despite my role as an active parent.
  4. Legal accountability for Talicia Townsend and her husband for their role in alienating me from my children.
  5. Policy reform in the State of Ohio to prevent other fathers from experiencing similar constitutional violations.

VII. Conclusion

This case is about more than just financial restitution—it is about justice for my children, for myself, and for every parent who has been unfairly denied their rights.

For years, I was subjected to unconstitutional treatment, financial coercion, and alienation from my children due to the actions of Talicia Townsend, her husband, and the State of Ohio.

Now that my children are approaching adulthood, it is time to set the record straight and hold those responsible accountable for their actions.

This is not just my fight—it is a fight for parental equality and justice.
