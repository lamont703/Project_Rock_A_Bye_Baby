The Meaning Behind "Rock A Bye Baby"

The nursery rhyme "Rock-a-bye baby" tells of a cradle that falls when the wind blows. This haunting metaphor perfectly captures what happens when the very institutions meant to protect children become the instruments of their emotional destruction.

"Rock-a-bye baby, in the treetop,
When the wind blows, the cradle will rock,
When the bough breaks, the cradle will fall,
And down will come baby, cradle and all."

In our case, the "wind" is systemic corruption, financial incentives, and parental alienation. The "bough" represents a family court system that has broken under the weight of its own contradictions. And the falling "cradle" symbolizes the destruction of parent-child bonds that should be sacred and protected.

But this project is also about catching that falling cradle‚Äîabout documenting the truth, demanding accountability, and working to build a system that truly serves children's best interests.

A Personal Letter from Lamont Evans

Why I created this platform and what it means to me personally

Dear Visitor,

If you're reading this, you've found your way to a story that I never wanted to tell, but one that must be told. For 17 years, I have lived through a nightmare that no parent should ever endure‚Äîwatching as a corrupt system systematically destroyed the relationships between me and my three children.

I am not a politician, activist, or legal scholar. I am simply a father who believed that love, dedication, and the constitutional guarantee of due process would be enough to protect my family. I was wrong.

This platform exists because silence has failed. Working within the system has failed. Trusting that justice would eventually prevail has failed. What hasn't failed is my love for my children and my belief that they deserve better than what this broken system has given them.

Every document you'll find here is real. Every timeline entry represents actual events that happened to real people‚Äîto my children and to me. This isn't political rhetoric or abstract policy discussion. This is what constitutional violations look like when they happen to your family.

I've created Project Rock A Bye Baby for three reasons:

  1. For my children: So they will one day know the truth about what happened and understand that their father never stopped fighting for meaningful relationships with them.
  2. For other parents: So those facing similar injustices will know they are not alone and will have resources to help them navigate this broken system.
  3. For systemic change: So that public awareness and legal accountability can finally force reform of a system that profits from family destruction.

The courts failed my children. The enforcement agencies failed my children. But I refuse to fail them by staying silent. Every child deserves meaningful relationships with both parents. Every parent deserves due process and equal protection under the law. These aren't radical ideas‚Äîthey're constitutional guarantees that have been systematically violated.

If you're a parent facing similar struggles, know that your pain is real, your fight matters, and you are not alone. If you're someone who cares about justice and children's welfare, I ask for your support in demanding accountability and reform.

The story documented here spans 17 years, but it's not over. The evidence I've compiled represents not just my fight for justice, but a fight for every child who has been failed by this system. Together, we can ensure that no other family endures what mine has endured.

Thank you for taking the time to learn the truth. Thank you for caring about justice. And thank you for helping to amplify a story that too many people would prefer remained hidden.

With determination and hope for change,
Lamont Evans
Founder, Project Rock A Bye Baby

Our Core Values
